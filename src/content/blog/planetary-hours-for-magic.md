# Planetary Hours and Days for Magic: A Practitioner's Guide

**The alignment of magical workings with planetary hours is one of the oldest and most enduring practices in the Western esoteric tradition.** From the temple priests of ancient Babylon to the grimoire authors of the Renaissance, practitioners have recognized that timing a ritual to coincide with the appropriate planetary influence can dramatically shape its outcome. Whether you practice ceremonial magic, folk witchcraft, rootwork, or any other tradition that acknowledges celestial influence, understanding the planetary hours system gives you a structured, reliable framework for choosing when to work.

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the planetary hours system as it applies to magical practice — historical context, planet-by-planet correspondences, practical timing strategies, and ethical considerations. ## Historical Context: Planetary Magic Through the Ages

### The Hellenistic Foundation

The planetary hours system originated in Hellenistic Egypt and Mesopotamia, where astrologer-priests developed the Chaldean Order — Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon — as a ranking of the seven visible celestial bodies by their apparent orbital speed. This ordering became the backbone of astrological timing for both mundane and magical purposes.

In the Greek Magical Papyri (PGM), a collection of Greco-Egyptian magical texts dating from the 2nd century BCE to the 5th century CE, practitioners routinely specified the planetary hour and day for ritual operations. A love invocation might specify "in the hour of Venus on the day of Venus," while a binding spell could call for "the hour of Saturn on the day of Saturn." These texts represent some of the earliest surviving evidence of systematic magical timing based on planetary hours.

### Medieval and Renaissance Grimoire Tradition

The tradition of planetary timing reached its fullest development in the grimoires of medieval and Renaissance Europe. Texts like the *Picatrix* (originally the Arabic *Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm*, 10th–11th century), the *Key of Solomon* (attributed to King Solomon but composed in the Italian Renaissance), and Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa's *Three Books of Occult Philosophy* (1533) all treat the planetary hours as fundamental to effective magical practice.

Agrippa's work is particularly influential. He devoted extensive chapters to the correspondences between planets, materials, colors, incenses, and types of magical operations. His system — which draws on Neoplatonic philosophy, Hermetic theory, and practical experimentation — remains the basis for most modern planetary magic.

The *Key of Solomon* goes further, specifying that certain talismans and pentacles can only be created during specific planetary hours on specific days. This dual-layer timing (day + hour) represents the gold standard of magical timing in the Western tradition.

### Renaissance to Modern Practice

The Renaissance magi — Marsilio Ficino, Giordano Bruno, John Dee — all incorporated planetary hours into their practice. Ficino's *De Vita Coelitus Comparanda* (1489) describes how to "draw down" planetary influences through timed rituals involving appropriate colors, scents, music, and materials.

In modern practice, planetary hours remain central to ceremonial magic, Wicca, hoodoo, and many eclectic traditions. The underlying principle is the same as it was two thousand years ago: by timing your working to align with the planetary energy that corresponds to your goal, you add the force of cosmic sympathy to your intention.

## Planet-by-Planet Guide for Magical Workings

### Sun Hour Magic

The [Sun](/blog/sun-hour-guide) governs visibility, authority, and the vital force. Sun hour is the time for magical workings that seek to illuminate, heal, empower, or elevate.

**Best workings during Sun hour:**
- Healing spells, especially for vitality and chronic conditions
- Spells for career success, promotion, and public recognition
- Rituals of self-empowerment and confidence
- Workings to gain favor from authority figures
- Illumination rituals — seeking truth, clarity, or spiritual insight
- Consecrating solar talismans and amulets

**Double power:** Sun hour on [Sunday](/blog/sunday-sun-day) creates the strongest solar magical window of the week.

### Moon Hour Magic

The [Moon](/blog/moon-hour-guide) rules the hidden realm — intuition, dreams, psychic ability, and the subconscious. Moon hour is the premier time for divination, dreamwork, and any working that calls upon the deeper waters of the mind.

**Best workings during Moon hour:**
- Divination of all kinds — tarot, scrying, runes, pendulum work
- Dream magic and lucid dreaming incubation
- Spells for enhancing psychic ability and intuition
- Protection spells for travelers (the Moon traditionally governs safe journeys)
- Fertility magic and rituals related to the menstrual cycle
- Emotional healing and shadow work
- Working with lunar phases (combining Moon hour with the appropriate phase amplifies the effect)

**Double power:** Moon hour on [Monday](/blog/monday-moon-day) maximizes lunar influence.

### Mars Hour Magic

Mars is the warrior planet — aggressive, protective, and fearless. [Mars hour](/blog/mars-hour-guide) is the time for magical workings that require force, confrontation, or the breaking of obstacles.

**Best workings during Mars hour:**
- Protection and shielding spells
- Banishing rituals — removing unwanted influences, people, or energies
- Spells for courage and strength in the face of adversity
- Competitive magic — gaining an edge in contests, litigation, or disputes
- Breaking hexes and reversing malefic work
- Workings related to surgery or physical healing that requires cutting
- Sexual magic focused on passion and raw desire

**Caution:** Mars energy is volatile. Workings during Mars hour can be more powerful but also more difficult to control. Beginners should work with Sun or Jupiter hour for related goals until they develop sufficient experience.

**Double power:** Mars hour on [Tuesday](/blog/tuesday-mars-day) creates peak martial energy.

### Mercury Hour Magic

[Mercury](/blog/mercury-hour-guide) is the messenger, the trickster, and the scholar. Mercury hour favors magic that involves words, knowledge, communication, and nimble thinking.

**Best workings during Mercury hour:**
- Divination (especially intellectual systems like astrology and numerology)
- Spells for eloquence, persuasion, and communication
- Workings to pass exams, enhance study, or gain new skills
- Commerce and trade magic — attracting customers, closing deals
- Trickster magic and glamours
- Consecrating divinatory tools (tarot decks, pendulums, rune sets)
- Sigil creation and word-based spell construction

**Double power:** Mercury hour on [Wednesday](/blog/wednesday-mercury-day) provides the strongest Mercurial current for magical work.

### Jupiter Hour Magic

[Jupiter](/blog/jupiter-hour-guide) is the Greater Benefic — the planet of abundance, expansion, and divine favor. Jupiter hour is the most auspicious time for workings aimed at increasing wealth, luck, wisdom, and spiritual connection.

**Best workings during Jupiter hour:**
- Prosperity and abundance spells
- Luck magic and fortune-drawing rituals
- Spells for legal success and just outcomes
- Workings for spiritual growth, initiation, and connection with higher wisdom
- Blessing rituals for individuals, spaces, or objects
- Spells for travel safety and expansion of horizons
- Charitable and devotional magic

**Double power:** Jupiter hour on [Thursday](/blog/thursday-jupiter-day) creates the single most fortunate magical window of the week — traditionally the best time for any working seeking benevolent results.

### Venus Hour Magic

[Venus](/blog/venus-hour-guide) is the planet of love, beauty, and harmony. Venus hour is the supreme time for love magic and any working that seeks to attract, beautify, or harmonize.

**Best workings during Venus hour:**
- Love spells of all kinds — attraction, reconciliation, deepening bonds
- Friendship magic and spells for social harmony
- Beauty and glamour magic
- Spells for artistic inspiration and creative success
- Workings to attract luxury, comfort, and material pleasure
- Self-love and self-worth rituals
- Consecrating love talismans, beauty charms, and copper amulets

**Double power:** Venus hour on [Friday](/blog/friday-venus-day) is the definitive window for love magic — recognized across virtually every tradition that employs planetary timing.

### Saturn Hour Magic

[Saturn](/blog/saturn-hour-guide) is the Great Malefic in traditional astrology, but in magical practice, Saturn's power is indispensable. Saturn governs boundaries, endings, time, discipline, and the deeper mysteries of death and rebirth. Saturn hour is the time for workings that require severity, restriction, or confrontation with difficult truths.

**Best workings during Saturn hour:**
- Binding spells — restricting harmful people or influences
- Banishing and exorcism
- Workings to end bad habits, toxic relationships, or stagnant situations
- Karmic and ancestral magic
- Protection through restriction and warding
- Necromantic and mediumship workings (Saturn rules the dead in Hellenistic tradition)
- Long-term magical operations that require patience and endurance
- Spells for discipline, focus, and commitment

**Caution:** Saturn's energy is heavy and constraining. Working with Saturn hour demands respect, clear intention, and a willingness to accept the consequences of invoking limitations. This is advanced work.

**Double power:** Saturn hour on [Saturday](/blog/saturday-saturn-day) concentrates Saturnian energy to its peak.

## Timing Strategies: Day + Hour Combinations

### The Double-Power Principle

The most effective magical timing combines the planetary day with the planetary hour. When both the day and the hour are ruled by the same planet, the energy is doubly concentrated. This "double-power" principle is the gold standard of planetary magical timing:

| Day | Hour | Combined Energy | Ideal Workings |
|-----|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Sunday | Sun hour | Double Sun | Healing, success, authority |
| Monday | Moon hour | Double Moon | Divination, dreams, intuition |
| Tuesday | Mars hour | Double Mars | Protection, banishing, courage |
| Wednesday | Mercury hour | Double Mercury | Communication, divination, study |
| Thursday | Jupiter hour | Double Jupiter | Prosperity, luck, blessings |
| Friday | Venus hour | Double Venus | Love, beauty, harmony |
| Saturday | Saturn hour | Double Saturn | Binding, endings, discipline |

### Sympathetic Day + Complementary Hour

When the double-power window does not align with your schedule, you can use a sympathetic combination — choosing a day and hour ruled by planets that support the same goal through different channels.

**Examples:**
- **Love working:** Venus hour on Monday (Venus love + Moon emotional depth) or Moon hour on Friday (Moon bonding + Venus romance day)
- **Prosperity working:** Jupiter hour on Sunday (Jupiter abundance + Sun success) or Sun hour on Thursday (Sun authority + Jupiter day of fortune)
- **Protection working:** Mars hour on Saturday (Mars active defense + Saturn restriction) or Saturn hour on Tuesday (Saturn binding + Mars day of strength)

### Avoiding Conflicting Energies

Some day-hour combinations create tension rather than harmony. A love spell during Saturn hour, for example, introduces constricting energy into a working that seeks openness and attraction. While experienced practitioners may intentionally use conflicting energies for specific purposes (binding a lover, ending an unwanted attraction), beginners should stick to harmonious combinations.

## Practical Ritual Planning with Planetary Hours

### Step 1: Define Your Intention

Before consulting the [Planetary Hours Calculator](/), clearly articulate what your working is meant to accomplish. The more specific your intention, the more precisely you can match it to the appropriate planetary energy.

### Step 2: Identify the Primary Planet

Using the correspondence table above, determine which planet most closely aligns with your goal. If your goal spans multiple planetary domains (for example, a prosperity spell that also involves communication with a business partner), choose the primary planet for the hour and a secondary planet for the day.

### Step 3: Find the Optimal Window

Use the [Planetary Hours Calculator](/) to locate the next occurrence of your chosen planetary hour. For maximum power, look for double-power windows (matching day and hour). If no double-power window is available within your timeline, select the best sympathetic combination.

### Step 4: Prepare Materials

Gather materials that correspond to your chosen planet — colors, metals, herbs, incenses, and candles as specified in the correspondence table. Wearing clothing in the planetary color, burning the associated incense, and working on an altar cloth of the appropriate color all reinforce the planetary resonance.

### Step 5: Begin the Working

Start your ritual or spell at the beginning of the chosen planetary hour, not before. Many practitioners find that the first few minutes of a planetary hour carry the freshest and most potent energy. Aim to complete the core of your working — the invocation, the spell casting, the talisman consecration — within the planetary hour itself.

### Step 6: Record and Review

Keep a magical journal documenting the planetary hour and day you chose, the materials used, the specific working performed, and the results observed over the following days and weeks. This record becomes an invaluable personal reference over time.

## Advanced Considerations

### Planetary Hours and Lunar Phases

For practitioners who work with both planetary hours and lunar phases, combining the two systems creates a triple layer of timing. A love spell cast during Venus hour on Friday during a waxing moon (for growth and attraction) carries three reinforcing energies. A banishing spell during Saturn hour on Saturday during a waning moon (for decrease and removal) is similarly amplified.

### Planetary Hours and Astrological Transits

Advanced practitioners may also consider the current astrological transit of the planet whose hour they are using. A Jupiter hour when Jupiter is transiting a beneficial sign (Sagittarius, Pisces, Cancer) carries more potent energy than a Jupiter hour when Jupiter is in a challenging sign (Gemini, Virgo, Capricorn). The planetary hours system functions independently of transit astrology, but layering these systems can deepen your practice.

### Planetary Hours and Elemental Correspondences

Each planet also has elemental associations that can inform your choice of magical tools and techniques:

- **Fire:** Sun, Mars — candle magic, fire scrying, written petitions burned in flame
- **Water:** Moon, Venus — bath rituals, potion making, water scrying, emotional release
- **Air:** Mercury, Jupiter — incantation, breath work, incense magic, spoken charms
- **Earth:** Saturn — buried spells, crystal work, graveyard magic, physical talismans

## Ethical Considerations

The planetary hours system is a neutral timing tool. Like astrology itself, it describes cosmic patterns and energies — it does not dictate how those energies should be used. As with any powerful tool, ethical responsibility rests with the practitioner.

Traditional magical ethics — varied as they are across traditions — generally recommend:

- **Clarity of intention.** Know exactly what you are asking for and why.
- **Proportionality.** Use the minimum force necessary for the desired result.
- **Respect for free will.** Love spells and influence workings carry particular ethical weight. Consider whether your working respects the autonomy of others.
- **Acceptance of consequences.** Saturn teaches that every action has a consequence. Work with full awareness of what you are setting in motion.

This guide presents planetary hours as a timing system rooted in documented historical practice. Readers are encouraged to apply it within the ethical framework of their own tradition.
